The Champion

Summary

A character that fills any room. Their personality is almost as big as their sense of humor. Champions are loved by all.

The Champion is a Physique-prime, 2-handed weapons user with a gregarious, encouraging attitude. Their Personality is Innovative, Harmonious, and Spontaneous.

Strategy

The strategy for a Champion should feel a bit wild. Champions make decisions quickly and often think out of the box. In a fight, they are usually the first to charge in and have a strong initial sense of who is good and who is bad.

Champions will swing hard and wide and try to cause as much chaos among enemy ranks as possible, often at great personal risk.

While they might appear hot-headed, their love of their comrades is what can keep them from making rash decisions. If it is made clear to them that they could be making decisions that endanger their friends, they can be persuaded to slow down and exercise caution.

Champions are encouraging and optimistic and bring this into social interactions whenever possible.

Description

Champions are action-oriented idealists. They are invested in abstract concepts of justice, harmony, and are incredible social-innovators. Champions are characters that concern themselves with exploring ideas, helping others, and creating alternative ways of doing things and solving problems.

Champions are good at going with the flow, so to speak, but find it difficult to execute plans that are developed too far ahead of time. They prefer to remain available so that they can respond and react with more flexibility to problems or interruptions that can arise.

When in groups, Champions love new ideas but can get frustrated if strategy or deliberation come at the cost of opportunities for action.

In pop culture, Kamina (Gurren Lagann) and Haruhi Suzumiya (The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzimiya) are both strong Champions in their personality. Other solid examples are Sailor Moon (Sailor Moon) and Naruto Uzumaki (Naruto ShippÅ«den). Real-life people include Brie Larsen and Che Guevara.

The Charmer

Summary

Charmers are truly free spirits. They exude sexual power whether or not they know it. Fluid and unbound, they are the envy of many.

The Charmer is a Prestige-prime archer with a graceful approach and even more charm than the Champion. Their Personality is Conventional, Harmonious, and Spontaneous.

Strategy

The strategy for a Charmer should be to avoid being trapped, held down, or oppressed in any way. The Charmer seeks their own freedom whenever possible, but will work for the betterment of others as much as they can too.

Contrary to the Champion, the Charmer is not an idealist. They calculate risk more readily than a Champion does and are less willing to sacrifice themselves for others. While this might appear selfish at first, a Charmer might respond with, âwell I canât help anyone if I am dead.â

In combat, the Charmer will sit back so they can have a good handle of the battlefield. They attack, scan, inform, and encourage all from a distance.

Charmers make sure their good deeds donât go unnoticed, because it is better for their survival if they are recognized as a do-gooder.

Description

Charmers are the captivators of the world. Known for having a dramatic and engaging personality, Charmers are always noticed when they are in social situations.

Charmers, not known for their patience or analytical rigor, make up for this in their grace and influential behavior.

Charmers live to be known and recognized and to experience what others have not (or lack the bravery to do).

Seen often as the most daring, Charmers are capable of going places that others would not be willing, making them incredible trailblazers, free-spirits, and overall memorable characters.

Examples

In pop culture, Merida (Brave) and Aladdin (Aladdin) are both strong Charmers in their personality. Other solid examples are Harley Quinn (DC Extended Universe) and Pippin Took (The Lord of the Rings). Real-life people include Katy Perry and Steve Irwin.

The Fighter

Summary

Fighters are ferocious entities. They stick to their values with a burning ambition. Many fear to challenge their will.

The Fighter is a Prowess-prime master of weapons with a stubborn, practical, and calculated way about them. Their Personality is Conventional, Rational, and Deliberate.

Strategy

The strategy for a Fighter should be calculated. Fighters are less interested in winning or losing so much as they are interested in being ârightâ and doing whatever maximizes the potential for the ârightâ outcome they have invisioned.

They excel at careful strategy as well as following through with their plans and taking action when it matters. They always have contingencies in case things donât go as planned.

Fighters are both courageous and stubborn and are not typically good with people. Despite this, their deeds can be heroic enough to win others over, even if they themselves are rough around the edges.

Practically speaking, in combat Fighters use small strikes against vital areas of their target until they make contact. At that point, they switch up their style and begin to hit brutally hard.

Description

Fighters are keen, sharp, and unwavering. Often seen as rigid, fierce, and stoic, Fighters are typically the most intense and skeptical of all the personalities.

While not necessarily innovative thinkers, they are incredibly thorough, letting no stone go unturned and no piece left unconsidered.

Fighters believe in order and self-control. They strictly adhere to rules, whether their own or societyâs. This gives them a sense of organization and peace when facing the world.

Fighters are capable of compartmentalizing information and handling a crisis situation better than any other. Fighters are motivated by a need to restore order from chaos, exact justice, and prepare for disaster.

The Mystic

Summary

Mystics are the enlightened. They live by the idea that a true leader is one who endures. They overcome disharmony through willpower.

The Mystic is a Nerve-prime practitioner of religious magics, possessing steely calm and an iron fist. Their Personality is Innovative, Harmonious, and Spontaneous.

Strategy

The strategy for a Mystic is sometimes to fight, but more often it involves enduring enemies long enough to show them compassion. A Mysticâs approach should be to continually and stubbornly insist that bridges can be built between friends and foes. Their idealism is unyielding.

As a last resort, the Mystic is capable of combat. They are trained in hand-to-hand and magic, and have many creative ways of supporting other magic users or even dissuading enemy magic.

Their melee strategy is especially terrifying, as they will use a flurry of damageless touches in order to find a weakspot. Once they make contact with this vulnerable area, they make a powerful, decisive attack of magic and fist.

They are incredibly hard to attack due to their ability to catch all physical attacks, and the combination of their Spell Resist plus Ebb (at higher levels). It is a good tactic for the Mystic to put themselves in danger in order to divert attacks.

Description

Mystics are as loving as they are eccentric. Their philosophy is that the most powerful influence one can have comes by surrendering oneâs own ambitions and plans and finding the flow that moves around them.

Mystics are highly adaptive and deeply idealistic characters. They place great value on harmony, openness, and boldness. Their courage and willpower is almost as impressive as the vast love they have for others.

Mystics are likely to deconstruct dichotomies like âfriendâ and âenemy.â They may be able to convert darkness to the light when others would never have ventured into such a place. They firmly believe that a better world is possible, if only all could endure the pain of the present together.

The Sage

Summary

Sages are founts of knowledge. They have a shrewed mind, piercing insight, and give solid advice. They seem wise beyond their years.

The Sage is an Erudition-prime, Alchemist, Scholar, or Chandler-licensed wizard full of eccentricities. Their Personality is Innovative, Harmonious, and Deliberate.

Strategy

Sages are primarily non-combative and have no direct offensive or defensive strategy in a fight. They will primarily support their allies and offer insight about how to overcome their opponents.

The majority of a Sageâs activity should be spent acquiring books and reading, using Skim or Study (basic maneuvers for Inscription). Acquiring many languages is important to Sages, so that they can read as many books as possible.

Sages play a key role in any party for this reason: they unlock the secrets of Lore. This allows a party to adapt and make the best decisions possible when encountering new things.

Sages are also quite tactical in their use of Mystical storage, their buffing spells, and their ability to wait as long as possible in combat before taking a turn. This is a good idea, because it gives them the best chance of making a strong initial Scan/Survey of any opponents.

Description

Sages are unmatched in their wit and wisdom that they bring to the world. They are even-keeled characters, but often burdened by the heaviness of the knowledge they carry.

Most sages are passionate teachers and share their insights with others freely.

They are idealistic and believe that most problems arise from a lack of understanding. They are driven by a desire to continually learn.

Despite seeming esoteric, obtuse, or intense at first, they are often capable of warmth and wholesomeness as one gets to know them. They gladly share their knowledge so that others can grow.

Examples

In pop culture, Dumbledore (Harry Potter) and Obi-Wan Kenobi (Star Wars) are both strong Sages in their personality. Other solid examples are Iroh (Avatar: The Last Airbender) and The Oracle (The Matrix). Real-life people include Bob Ross and bell hooks.

The Specialist

Summary

Specialists are true survivors. Nothing can hold a specialist back or keep them down. Specialists always improvise and overcome.

The Specialist is a Grace-prime sneak using devious tactics and deadly weapons with a bit of cunning thrown in. Their Personality is Conventional, Resolute, and Spontaneous.

Strategy

The strategy for a Specialist is to avoid being seen and to avoid negative consequences for their actions whenever possible. In very practical terms, they will use Stealth (basic maneuver) often, whether in combat or not.

Their solution to most problems is simply to avoid dealing with the problem directly. Specialists will take this approach when fighting, solving puzzles, or engaging in social affairs.

When Specialists fight, they make sure that every hit counts. They will hit hard and do what they can to avoid being hit back or caught.

Description

Specialists are among the most efficient and effective people, whatever they set their mind to.

While not necessarily brave or good with people, Specialists invest themselves instead into their own abilities.

Specialists are the most talented and driven of all the personalities, seeking first and foremost to make themselves and their work of the most excellent quality.

Specialists, while sometimes seen as self-serving, are often valuable members of any team due to their flexibility and versatility. They are vital in a crisis or dangerous situation, because of their ability to strike the problem at its heart (or at least get away).

Examples

In pop culture, Arya Stark (Game of Thrones) and Black Widow (Marvel Cinematic Universe) are both strong Specialists in their personality. Other solid examples are Katniss Everdeen (The Hunger Games) and Han Solo (Star Wars). Real-life people include Bear Grylls and Bruce Lee.

The SpellSlinger

Summary

Spellslingers are the cleverest beings. They are brilliant, unpredictable, and utterly unique. Ideas mean everything to them.

The Spellslinger is a Wits-prime, unlicensed or Mage-licensed wizard with a penchant for getting into trouble. Their Personality is Innovative, Resolute, and Spontaneous.

Strategy

Tactically speaking, Spellslingers are the most powerful combatants out of every persona by a significant margin. However due to the toll their spells take on their Sanity, it is important that the Spellslingers pace themselves and reserve their strongest abilities for the right moments.

In very practical terms, they have a devastating two-move combo that they regularly employ: freeze the legs, then melt. This is used against dangerous opponents at a distance and can cripple almost anything.

Spellslingers use their cleverness to cast spells, as opposed to their knowledge of portaling. This gives them incredible flexibility. But this also means that they lack Lore-related versatility like identifying. They make up for their lack of wisdom with unbeatable keenness, cleverness, craftiness, and tactical genius. They pair well with a book-smart sort of persona.

Description

Spellslingers are daunting and often misunderstood people of brilliance.

The most innovative and eccentric of all the personas, Spellslingers are capable of coming up with wild and absurd ideas on the fly, thinking completely out of the box and disregarding plans or expectations at a momentâs notice (if a better idea presents itself).

Often divergent, Spellslingers prefer to avoid the complications that can arise out of blunt, straightforwardness. Spellslingers instead tend to find alternatives that no one thought existed or were even possible.

Spellslingers, while cursed with an eternal inability to commit themselves to finishing things, are often more fluid and flexible than anyone else. This allows them to take risks and choose paths that few would ever dare to.

The Synergist

Summary

Synergists are daunting personas. They are rational, relentless, and meticulous. To them, every action is a step toward their goals.

The Synergist is a Nerve-prime knight with a Paladin or Templar magic license in pursuit of dramatic ambitions. Their Personality is Innovative, Resolute, and Deliberate.

Strategy

Synergists are focused on thinking about what really matters in a given situation and developing a strategy that can see that plan through. Synergists rarely think about deficiencies or shortcomings, as long as those barriers can be solved through technological or magical means.

Synergists tend to think about the big picture, and they use their considerable wisdom and knowledge to focus on the best way to maximize their efforts.

They are incredibly stubborn and often self-righteous. In a group or party, both sides will have to work together to make sure that the Synergist feels valued but isnât overwhelming the other members of the group with too much criticism or strategizing. They have a habit of being extra.

In combat, Synergists are unbelievably tough. They have armor, healing, and high Spell Resist (coupled with Counter Spell). Their moves hit very hard and they can enable other Spellcasters near them to hit hard as well.

Description

Synergists are the most complex persona of all. While they rely heavily on their relentless drive and capacity for knowledge, they never choose the easy way out. They are committed to whatever path is has the best consequences. They hone their mind and let magic or technology enhance their physical capabilities.

Synergists are the most inwardly driven of all personas, committing themselves to improvement constantly. They take this attitude with the world around them as well. Nothing is good enough, which is why they have so much work to do. They believe a better world is possible through careful and calculated execution.

While Synergists tend to struggle when it comes to convincing others to do their bidding, they are notoriously unmoving in their own convictions. Sometimes their committed drive alone is enough to sway others to their cause.

Examples

In pop culture, Ellen Ripley (Aliens) and Yennefer of Vengerberg (The Witcher) are both strong Synergists in their personality. Other solid examples are Batman (DC Comics) and Imperator Furiosa (Mad Max: Fury Road). Real-life people include Nikola Tesla and Friedrich Nietzsche.

The Tank

Summary

Tanks are the foundation beneath us all. Tanks are steady yet full of passion and their loyalty never wavers.

The Tank is a Wellness-prime being of grit and metal with a steady and compassionate philosophy to life. Their Personality is Conventional, Harmonious, and Deliberate.

Strategy

The strategy for a Tank is to protect. In everything they do, they stand between the edge of chaos and the ones they love. Their duty is to make sure that those they love can stay safe and undisturbed, so that they will be empowered to do their own work too.

Tanks operate on an assumption of trust: that if they keep others safe from chaos, those they protect will use their freedom to do whatever they need to do. Without this mutual relationship and teamwork, Tanks would surely perish (and tragically so). They rely on others as much as others rely on them.

In combat, Tanks distract enemies and absorb blows so that their teammates can end matters quickly. Tanks rely on blocking attacks with a shield, which is the most-reliable and lowest-cost form of Defense. But blocking comes at the cost of a sore or even wounded shield arm. Tanks often spend time in recovery, for this reason.

Description

Tanks are the reliable, sturdy, humble folk of the world, and spend much of their time diligently working to keep things from falling apart.

Tanks are always the most unthanked people of any story. Tanks do work that most others donât have the patience for and are rarely given the credit they deserve. They donât do hard work because of the work itself, but because of the kind of world they believe their work is helping to build.

Tanks are not known for their creative capabilities but are seen as trustworthy and reliable people. They show up on time and make sure everyone gets a chance to speak. Their ethos, not their wit, is why so many are given positions of power and leadership.

Tanks are the sort that will serve others because they believe it is their duty to, and they expect that others will respond mutually in kind.

The Vicar

Summary

Vicars are fearsome individuals. They are brilliant, demanding, and intense. They can envision a whole universe in their minds.

The Vicar is a Prestige-prime, Officiant or Priest licensed wizard interested in the pursuits of fame, truth, and order. Their Personality is Innovative, Resolute, and Deliberate.

Strategy

Compared to Synergists, who seek to enhance themselves in order to succeed, Vicars are concerned with enhancing their environment instead. Vicars use their powerful presence to influence people (and even the fabric of reality) to do their bidding.

Vicars are rarely interested in doing any fighting, as it is much better to have a plan or allies do this sort of thing instead. Vicars are masters when it comes to overpowering their opponents, even before the fighting begins. They rely on both quantity and quality of allies.

Vicars are capable of spying on others and causing chaos among opponents in combat. The strong Vicars can even bend reality and have regional turf where they are more influential. They are very dangerous when left unchecked.

Description

Vicars are indimidating geniuses. Logical, persistent, and exacting, Vicars have a plan and they need others in order to see things through.

Vicars concern themselves with understanding and innovating complex systems of power and structures of control. They are unmatched at formulating abstract strategies at a large scale and finding the right people to do whatever job needs to be done as part of their plan.

Vicars are skilled at getting others to believe in their ideas, often gaining both intense followers and intense enemies.

Always ready to overcome even the most impossible challenges, Vicars never face problems alone. The Vicarâs ambitions always bring loyal believers to join their cause.

Examples

In pop culture, Gandalf (The Lord of the Rings) and Azula (Avatar: The Last Airbender) are both strong Vicars in their personality. Other solid examples are Light Yagami (Death Note) and Carmilla (Castlevania). Real-life people include Malcom X and Katharine Hepburn.
