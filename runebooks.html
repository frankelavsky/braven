Alchemst License

The Alchemist is given a Pin (a palm-sized brooch) in the outline of equilateral triangle and an Enchiridion (book of runes). The Enchiridion has a chain that loops between either end of the spine and can be attached to a belt or strap for quick use. The book will also track anyone who touches it or looks upon it when it is open. The runes written inside cannot be copied by any known spell, making this book one of a select few that remain.

This license was earned because the character attended Goldwynne University and researched Alchemy or a sub-field of alchemy, wrote and defended a thesis, and graduated. If there were no available Alchemist’s Enchiridions when the character graduated, they had to wait for one to become available before becoming licensed.

As part of the licensure, alchemists are expected to use their license with a profession such as blacksmithing, tinkering, leatherworking, or professional alchemy (potion crafting and transmutation) for Magisterium purposes. Alchemists must pay taxes to the Magisterium equal to 10% of their earnings and give the Magisterium a 50% discount on official orders. If the Alchemist does not wish to be a professional, they are expected to regularly publish research (like a scholar or naturalist).

Alchemists must occasionally undergo a License Review (typically in order to receive new runes or elevate their Class Rank). This happens at a Magisterium Office or Outpost and typically only takes several minutes. They must surrender their book and brooch to the Officiant operating the office. These items get teleported to the Magisterium headquarters for review. Generally, the review is meticulous. There is only about a 5% chance that a violation will go unnoticed. (An unauthorized person using the enchiridion is the most common and significant violation.) The player or user may spend a Plot Point per use to say that the Magisterium was having a busy day with reviews and simply missed the violation.

If a single violation is found, the Enchiridion will be returned with a warning.

If the character has accrued up to 2 violations since their last review, the Enchiridion will be returned but any requested Class Elevation will be denied. The character must wait for 5 levels until another review can be made.

If the character has had violations on three subsequent reviews or a single review with three violations, their Enchiridion and pin will not return.

They must travel to the Magisterium Headquarters in Lamoor, file an appeal, and undergo a court hearing to argue their innocence (risking imprisonment if found guilty).

Illegal: Sorcerer/ess,

(unlicensed)

The Sorceress does not hold a Magisterium License but has somehow acquired several, written runes in a book of their own. Perhaps they are a dropout student who stole a book, the child of a wizard who doesn’t care for the law, or a wizard that was excommunicated from the Magisterium for some reason. Whatever the back story, they have some runes that they have acquired and they are not a recognized, shaman, druid, or holy person with an Indigenous Practitioner’s permit.

If caught using magic by a Templar, Officiant, Ranger, or local law enforcement you may have an international bounty put on your head or even attacked on the spot (depending on the circumstances). However, some may not request to see your permit or license on their own volition.

The Sorceress does not learn new magic as they level up, since there is no formal regulation to how they acquire runes. Instead, the way that new runes are acquired must be negotiated with the Host. Typically this involves visiting a sagely teacher, finding lost tomes of knowledge, or some other narrative means. New runes gained in this way cost 3, 5, or 10 Plot Points, depending on whether the runes are Legal, Category 1 Illegal, or Category 2 Illegal.

During character creation, the player may choose up to 5 total runes from any category with the following limitations:

No more than 2 Category 1 Illegal runes.

No more than 1 Category 2 Illegal rune.

Indigenous Practitioner, (by Permit)

The Indigenous Practitioner does not hold a Magisterium License and did not learn their magic from the Magisterium’s University or Academy. Instead, the Indigenous Practitioner learned to use magic from their people’s traditions. The practitioner has received a permit to use their runes in accordance with any society that respects Magisterium Law.

When requested to by a Templar, Ranger, Officiant Wizard, or local law enforcement, an Indigenous Practitioner must show their permit in order to prove they are legally permitted to use magic. Like an Enchiridion, when put in contact with a Templar, Ranger, or Officiant badge, the permit will glow and produce the full name and legal registration number of the Templar, Ranger, or Officiant whose badge was used.

The Indigenous Practitioner does not learn new magic as they level up, since there is no formal regulation to how they acquire runes. Instead, the way that new runes are acquired must be negotiated with the Host. Typically this involves visiting a sagely teacher, finding lost tomes of knowledge, or some other narrative means. New runes gained in this way cost between 3 and 5 Plot Points, depending on whether the runes are Legal or Category 1 Illegal: Primordial.

During character creation, the player may choose any 5 Legal or Category 1: Primordial runes.

Scholar License

The Scholar is given a Pin (a palm-sized brooch) in the silhouette of a curled, sleeping wyvern and an Enchiridion (book of runes). The Enchiridion has a chain that loops between either end of the spine and can be attached to a belt or strap for quick use. The book will also track anyone who touches it or looks upon it when it is open. The runes written inside cannot be copied by any known spell, making this book one of a select few that remain.

This license was earned because the character attended Goldwynne University, researched a specific field of study (any could be chosen), wrote and defended a thesis, and graduated. Scholar’s Enchiridions are the most common but most wizards choose another license instead. Technically, anyone who graduated from Goldwynne University could opt for a Scholar License, even if they originally set out to get a different one. Often, if the wait is too long for the specific Enchiridion they want, they may opt for a Scholar License instead. If other Universities are lacking professors, Goldwynne can be pressured to incentivize wizards towards this path.

As part of the licensure, scholars are expected either to do lab work, teach as a professor at a university, curate museums, preside over archives or libraries, or (in rarer cases) do field work. Scholars are expected to be prolific writers, publishing at a minimum one book or volume every two years. Most commonly, Scholars work with the non-magical universities across Braven as professors or as a partner in the work of an Alchemist, Naturalist, or Healer.

Scholars must occasionally undergo a License Review (typically in order to receive new runes or elevate their Class Rank). This happens at a Magisterium Office or Outpost and typically only takes several minutes. They must surrender their book and brooch to the Officiant operating the office. Every templar is given a psychological evaluation to see if they are better suited as a Sentry or an Errant. At no point in history are Templar Enchiridions in short supply, so Templar are always given licensure upon attaining their Discipline.

As part of the licensure, templar must serve the Holy Body or the Magisterium as a Knight Errant or Knight Sentry. Errants are given Calls, which are quests that they must complete on behalf of the Holy Body or the Magisterium. Sentry positions are only given to the most trustworthy and reliable templar, while templar that are harder to keep reigns on are given Errant positions. Both are considered vital to the success and livelihood of the Holy Body and the Magisterium, like two edges of one blade. Templar of one kind refer to the other kind as their Sister Blade, meaning the other side of the same tool. Creating Powerful Combos

Creating custom moves is often about making combinations of gifts and spells and even character’s different advantages work in your favor. While basic attacks might not be considered a combo, some actually are! Even just using a bow or crossbow is always a two-step process: load and fire. Since there are two separate actions that are required, this is considered a combo attack by default. (Contrary to swinging a melee weapon, which only requires a single action.) Both of these two actions can be enhanced by other moves and abilities separately.

Combo attacks can be daunting at first. But with these examples and guide below, hopefully new players will get the hang of things in no time.

In Braven, almost every attack ability can be combined with another in some way.

But why combine things? Well sometimes it’s just to increase damage in a single attack, but other times there are tactical reasons why one would want to combine moves. We will go over the most important (and even some absurd) combinations in this section, starting with the easiest and moving into the more complicated. Here’s a preview of what’s to come:
