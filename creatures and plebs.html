Introduction

The Orange Book (or just Encounters) is a book of characters, creatures, and animals that can be used to build scenes in your story.

Some campaigns might be heavily based on fighting and combat. To that end, virtually everything in this book can be battled. But other campaigns might be more focused on mystery, thrills, intrigue, or just wholesome content. There are plenty of things in this book for those campaigns too.

The first section of this book is devoted to Personas. This section is dedicated to human characters created using Braven’s system (with a few balancing elements included). Before each Persona is introduced, a brief explanation, strategy advice, and real life (and pop culture) examples for that Persona are given. Personas should fill the world of your story, from the blacksmith in a town, to the a hired mercenary, secretary, love interest, and villainous priest. Of course, you are welcome to custom-cook your own characters! But these 11 are designed to give a broad array of distinct actors for your plot.

Following Personas is the bulk of content for this book: Creatures and Monsters. This section is meant to mimic a “field guide” - a compendium that scholars and wizards have sketched together to aid the denizens of Braven in identifying and dealing with all its critters. This section is slower to parse than the Personas; the creatures have Lore sections included. These sections give a sensory description, background, and then unlockable pieces of information that characters can learn as they progress in their knowledge.

After Creatures and Monsters is a small section for Ordinary Animals. These are things that exist in our world, generalized a bit for the purposes of Braven’s system (and balance).

As anyone who is reading this knows: there is so much more I hope to do before the official release of this volume! This is just a Beta version. So please, if you have any feedback, hopes, concerns, suggestions, or questions, just let me know. I am especially looking forward to the art, myself.

I hope you enjoy.
